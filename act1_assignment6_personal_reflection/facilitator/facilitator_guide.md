# Facilitator Guide — Assignment 6 Personal Reflection

## Learning Objective

Participants connect the talk to their real teams.

They should leave with at least one concrete repository rule they can implement.

## Recommended Timing

| Segment | Time |
|---|---:|
| Explain assignment | 2 min |
| Individual reflection | 6 min |
| Pair discussion | 8 min |
| Group closeout | 6 min |
| Debrief | 4 min |

Total: 26 minutes

## Compressed Conference Version

| Segment | Time |
|---|---:|
| Individual reflection | 3 min |
| Pair share | 3 min |
| Group closeout | 3 min |

Total: 9 minutes

## Setup Script

Say:

```text
This is where the talk becomes personal.
The question is not whether some company somewhere is doing vibe coding.
The question is where our own teams already delegate work without enough boundaries.
```

## Facilitation Prompts

Ask:

```text
What review comment do you repeat most often?
What production risk do you worry Claude could amplify?
What task would you let Claude do today?
What task would you never let Claude do without approval?
What should be written into CLAUDE.md before your team scales usage?
```

## What Strong Answers Look Like

Strong answers are:

```text
specific
actionable
repo-level
testable
connected to real review pain
clear about approval boundaries
```

Weak answers are:

```text
generic
aspirational
too broad
not enforceable
not tied to real code behavior
```

## Closing Message

The best architecture guidance is not only spoken in review meetings.

It is encoded into the repository.
